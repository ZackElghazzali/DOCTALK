PROPAGATOR demo scripts

Topology-aware MAS security

Small, runnable examples that show the point without dragging in the whole lab.

These demos mirror the documentation’s main ideas: represent a MAS as a directed graph, simulate adversarial spread with an SIR-style process, compute risk metrics, and show why topology only starts to matter once resistance heterogeneity becomes non-trivial. In other words: the graph is innocent until the agents become uneven.

What each demo covers

The scripts are intentionally small and self-contained. They don’t pretend to be the full framework; they expose the mechanics cleanly so someone can run them, inspect them, and then swap in the real PROPAGATOR modules with minimal drama.

demo_01_topology_spread.py

Spread across toy topologies

Compares chain, star, and layered DAG layouts under one attack. Shows infection curves, final infected fraction, and a toy PRS analogue.

Open script
demo_02_hst_threshold.py

Why heterogeneity matters

Sweeps node resistance variance and visualizes the topology spread gap. This is the documentation’s HST story, but with fewer ceremonial robes.

Open script
demo_03_guardrail_placement.py

Edge guardrail placement

Places guards on a small rewired graph and compares “guard the obvious hub” versus “guard the dangerous path.” Degree is useful, but not clairvoyant.

Open script
demo_04_message_interceptor.py

Message-level interception

Mocks an edge interceptor with lightweight SCC/SAC/CAPC-style checks to illustrate the guardrail idea without depending on live MAF plumbing.

Star

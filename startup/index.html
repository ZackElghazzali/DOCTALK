Move more, sit less: small steps that help

Regular movement reduces risk of chronic disease. Start with 10-minute walks after meals, use stairs where possible, and break up long sitting periods every 30–60 minutes. Aim for 150 minutes of moderate activity per week — it adds up.

Know your medicines: why adherence matters

Taking prescriptions as directed maximizes benefit and reduces complications. Use a single pharmacy when possible, set daily reminders, and review medicines with your clinician at each visit. Never stop a med abruptly without professional advice.

Vaccines: the basics for adults

Vaccinations (flu, COVID boosters, tetanus, shingles, and others based on age) protect you and your community. Check with your provider or local public health for up-to-date guidance and schedules.

Pregnancy planning and healthy behaviors

If you're pregnant or planning a pregnancy, stop smoking, limit alcohol, ensure folic acid intake, and discuss medications with your clinician. Early prenatal care improves outcomes for mother and baby.

January 5, 2026 · First Aid · 2 min read

When to see urgent care vs ER

Use urgent care for non-life-threatening issues (sprains, minor cuts, fever without severe symptoms). Go to the emergency room for chest pain, severe shortness of breath, stroke signs, heavy bleeding, or altered consciousness.
